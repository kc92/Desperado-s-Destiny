### Quality Calculation
- Base: Product quality chance
- Slot: +quality bonus %
- Worker: +skill/200 per worker
- Character: +crafting skill/200
- Random: Roll determines tier

## Rush Orders

**Available For**: Most crafted goods, some services
**Time Reduction**: 75% (to 25% of normal)
**Cost Multiplier**: 2.0x - 3.0x original cost

**Cannot Rush**: Breeding, crops, boarding, aged products

## Production Bonuses Stack

1. **Slot Speed Bonus** (10-50% from upgrades)
2. **Worker Skill Bonus** (up to 30% per worker)
3. **Worker Trait Bonus** (various)
4. **Multiple Worker Bonus** (15-30% per extra worker)
5. **Rush Bonus** (75% reduction if rushed)

**Example**:
- Base time: 240 minutes
- Slot bonus: 20% faster = 192 minutes
- Worker bonus: 25% faster = 144 minutes
- Multi-worker: 15% faster = 122 minutes
- Rush: 75% faster = 30 minutes

- **Ranch**: Milk, Corn (fast, low cost)

## Worker Optimization

### Minimum Viable Team
- Ranch: 3-4 workers (1 trainer, 2-3 laborers)
- Mine: 4-5 workers (1 prospector, 3-4 miners)
- Workshop: 3-4 specialists (1 each type)
- Saloon: 3-4 workers (bartender, cook, entertainer)
- Stable: 2-3 workers (trainer, breeder, hand)

### Optimal Team
- High skill (60+) specialist for each product type
- 1-2 forepeople for bonus
- Mix of efficiency + quality traits
- Backup workers for rotation

### Budget Team
- Skill 20-40 workers with no negative traits
- Focus on laborers (cheap, versatile)
- Train up slowly over time
- Accept lower quality/speed

## Time Management

### Active Sessions (1-2 hours)
- Start: Repairs, Horseshoes, Milk, Coal
- Collect frequently for immediate gold

### Medium Sessions (4-8 hours)
- Start: Most crafted goods, training, ore
- Set before work/sleep, collect on return

### Passive Income (24 hours)
- Start: Breeding, boarding, rooms
- Minimal management, steady income

### Rush Strategy
- Use for urgent needs or limited play time
- Best on high-margin products
- Calculate: Is 2-3x cost worth immediate result?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-hiring**: Too many workers = high wage costs
2. **Under-training**: Low skill = poor quality/yield
3. **Ignoring Morale**: Low morale = strikes/quits
4. **Wrong Specialists**: Need right worker for special products
5. **No Reserve Gold**: Can't pay wages = lose workers
6. **Ignoring Quality**: Higher quality = much better yield
7. **Rush Everything**: Expensive and often not needed
8. **No Auto-Sell**: Inventory bloat from uncollected goods
