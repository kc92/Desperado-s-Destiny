# The Three Factions

**Last Updated:** 2025-12-31

The Sangre Territory is contested by three distinct factions, each with their own culture, motivations, and vision for the frontier.

---

## Overview

| Faction | Alignment | Core Value | Territory |
|---------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Settler Alliance | Law & Order | Progress | Red Gulch, Railroad |
| Nahi Coalition | Spiritual Balance | Sacred Lands | Kaiowa Mesa, Canyon |
| Frontera | Outlaw Freedom | Survival | The Frontera, Badlands |

---

## Settler Alliance

> *"From sea to shining sea, this land is ours by divine right. Progress, civilization, and the American dream."*
> — Governor Edmund Ashford, 1875

### Philosophy
- **Manifest Destiny:** Belief in American expansion as divine right
- **Progress:** Railroads, mining, industry, civilization
- **Law & Order:** Establishing government and justice

### Culture
- American frontier settlers (1870s)
- Christian religious tradition
- Hierarchical class structure
- Emphasis on property rights and commerce

### In-Game
- Access to: Lawman abilities, railroad connections, government contracts
- Reputation with: Sheriffs, businessmen, settlers
- Enemies: Outlaws, "hostile natives"

**Detailed Lore:** [archive/lore/settler-alliance-culture.md](../archive/lore/settler-alliance-culture.md)

## Nahi Coalition

> *"The land remembers what we forget. The blood remembers what we deny."*
> — Elder Wise Sky, Keeper of the Old Ways

### Philosophy
- **Sacred Lands:** The Sangre Territory is spiritually significant
- **Preservation:** Protect ancestral grounds and traditions
- **Balance:** Harmony between physical and spiritual worlds

### Culture
- Coalition of three fictional nations: Kaiowa, Tseka, Nahi
- Shamanic spiritual tradition
- Council-based leadership
- Oral history and storytelling

### In-Game
- Access to: Spiritual abilities, vision quests, sacred knowledge
- Reputation with: Elders, shamans, spirit guides
- Enemies: Those who desecrate sacred sites

**Detailed Lore:** [archive/lore/nahi-coalition-culture.md](../archive/lore/nahi-coalition-culture.md)

## Frontera

> *"Out here, there's no law but what you make. No justice but what you take."*
> — El Tigre, Outlaw King

### Philosophy
- **Freedom:** Reject all external authority
- **Survival:** The strong endure, the clever prosper
- **Pragmatism:** Do what works, morality be damned

### Culture
- Outlaws, bandits, smugglers, outcasts
- Mexican-American border culture blend
- Loose honor codes among thieves
- Black market economy

### In-Game
- Access to: Criminal networks, contraband, assassination contracts
- Reputation with: Bandits, smugglers, fence operators
- Enemies: Lawmen, bounty hunters

**Detailed Lore:** [archive/lore/frontera-culture.md](../archive/lore/frontera-culture.md)

## Faction Relationships

```
                    SETTLER ALLIANCE
                         /    \
                   WAR  /      \  TENSE
                       /        \
        NAHI COALITION ———————— FRONTERA
                     UNEASY TRUCE
```

### Settler vs Nahi
- **Conflict:** Land rights, sacred sites, cultural survival
- **History:** Broken treaties, massacres, displacement
- **Present:** Ongoing territorial war

### Settler vs Frontera
- **Conflict:** Law vs outlawry
- **History:** Bandit raids, lynchings, corruption
- **Present:** Constant cat-and-mouse

### Nahi vs Frontera
- **Relationship:** Uneasy coexistence
- **Common Ground:** Both oppose Settler expansion
- **Tension:** Different values, occasional clashes
